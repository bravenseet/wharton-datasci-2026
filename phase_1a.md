Phase 1a Methodology — Power Rankings & Win Probabilities
League Table & Power Rankings
We built the league table and power rankings in R using a customised Elo rating system. Every team started at a baseline rating of 1500 and ratings were updated iteratively across all 1,312 games. For each game, we computed a home performance score based on a weighted blend of goal differential (45%) and xG differential (45%), with a small constant (0.1) to reflect home-ice advantage. This score was passed through a sigmoid function to produce a continuous performance outcome between 0 and 1, compared against the Elo-predicted expectation, and ratings were updated using a K-factor of 30.
By incorporating xG differential alongside goals, the rankings reflect underlying team quality rather than just results; a team that consistently outperforms expectations in shot generation is rewarded even if they ran into poor finishing luck on the scoreboard.
Win Probability Predictions
With final Elo ratings established, we used the rating differential between home and away teams as the sole feature to predict a binary home win outcome. We trained and evaluated both Logistic Regression and XGBoost on a 1,000-game training split, tested on the remaining 312 games.
XGBoost achieved around 58% accuracy on the test set, and we also observed cases where teams with clearly higher Elo ratings were assigned lower win probabilities; a sign that the model was overfitting to noise rather than learning a meaningful signal. With only a single input feature, XGBoost's flexibility becomes a liability. Logistic Regression, by contrast, fits a clean linear relationship on the log-odds scale, which is exactly what this problem structure calls for. Its AIC of around 1,300 against a null baseline of 1,500 confirms it is capturing genuine predictive signal. We used the logistic model's predictions for the final submission.
