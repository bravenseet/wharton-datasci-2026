Phase 1b Methodology — Offensive Line Quality Disparity
We began by reshaping the raw game-level data from wide to long format, stacking home and away rows into a single unified table with columns for team, opponent, line label, xG, and TOI. Rows with missing values or zero TOI were dropped upfront to prevent infinite values in later calculations.
Rather than filtering to only first and second lines, we categorized "first_off" as First and everything else as Secondary — capturing the full picture of a team's top line versus all remaining offensive depth combined, which better reflects how line disparity actually manifests in hockey.
To account for defensive matchups, we computed each opponent's season-long xG allowed per 60 minutes as a defensive strength baseline. Each line's raw xG/60 was then divided by the opponent's defensive rating, producing an adjusted xG/60 that controls for the quality of competition faced. This adjustment means a line that posted strong numbers against tough defenses is credited more than one that dominated weak opponents.
We then aggregated using TOI-weighted averages, so that performance in high-minute situations carries more weight than limited sample appearances. A minimum TOI threshold of 300 minutes was applied to filter out lines with insufficient data. Finally, we computed the disparity ratio (First ÷ Secondary adjusted xG/60) and ranked teams by its log transformation, which handles skewed distributions more robustly and is better suited for ranking purposes.
The methodology was refined iteratively: after an initial version, we reviewed the logic and used AI tools to compare approaches and identify improvements, particularly around the opponent adjustment and aggregation steps. The final decisions on method and framing remained our own.
