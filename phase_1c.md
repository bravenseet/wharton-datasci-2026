Phase 1c Methodology — Data Visualization
To answer the commissioner's question, we plotted each team's Elo rating against their absolute log disparity ratio in a scatter plot with a linear regression trendline, merging the outputs from Phase 1a and 1b. Elo rating sits on the x-axis as the measure of overall team strength, and the log disparity ratio on the y-axis as the measure of offensive line imbalance, labelled explicitly so the chart tells its own story without additional explanation.
We used the log ratio rather than the raw ratio because it handles skew more cleanly and is more symmetric, making cross-team comparisons fairer. The shaded confidence band around the trendline makes the uncertainty in the relationship visible at a glance.
The downward slope is the central finding: stronger teams tend to have more balanced offensive lines. The wide confidence band, however, reminds the reader that this is a trend rather than a rule; some top-heavy teams still perform well, and the relationship is far from deterministic. The title "Does a Balanced Attack Matter?" frames the question before the reader interprets the data, and lets the chart answer it.
